About

First welcome to the frontpage, I appreciate you taking the time to review some of my work. My name is Brianda Hernandez. and I am an upcoming IT professional. Through my diverse life experiences of having been a collegiate soccer player, military service member and IT professional, my mindset has developed into that of a driven individual focused on accomplishing their goals. Currently, I work as the Tech Liaison of the Chief Executive Office (CEO) of a county government. My day-to-day responsibilities include coordinating with the IT department, such as software updates, hardware repairs, live meeting production assistance & digital workflow development. Outside of work, I spend my time with my significant other and two dogs, Whiskey & Tera. Thankfully because of COVID and the mandate to work from home I have been able to spend more quality time with them. The dogs love taking walks, but they are mostly indoor dogs, so the walks are short. Which leads me to your invitation, by keeping this short and sweet. So, without further ado please enjoy exploring the rest of my site.

My path as a web developer has not been linear or quick, in fact I did not even start out in IT. Before, I was a soccer player and coach. I taught all ages and positions, but I was mostly a goalkeeper coach. Now I did try to go pro but because of a concussion I was not able to sign that season and I had to find another path. Hence, my entrance into the military. Where I enlisted as a Cyber Transportation Specialist, pretty much a fancy way of saying I would be working in IT. This would be the start of my second love. When I started my tech (or job) school, I immediately fell in love with everything I was learning, including circuits, diodes, switches, routers, crypto and more. Unfortunately, when I got stationed at my first base, I mostly worked with long-range satellite technology, therefore I did not get to work with the day-to-day computer equipment. Then, after 3 years, my time with the military was over and I became a civilian again. Where almost immediately I enrolled in school to finish my Computer Science degree, continuing my education of IT. However, I got sidetrack by playing collegiate soccer for the school and taking the massive amount of general education courses. Then I got sidetracked again by transferring schools to City College of San Francisco (CCSF) and adding another degree. I added the Computer Networking & Information Technology degree to my list of degrees to get. It was a decision that was made with the idea of getting a job as an IT Support Specialist, instead of the administrative assistant positions I had been in. Which worked because I was hired by Nevada County as a temporary Computer Services Technician. Now up to this point I had no clue what I wanted to do in the IT field. I just knew that I wanted to be a part of it. So, this is where working for Nevada County was extremely helpful because they had a smaller IT department. Where I was exposed to or work with people in other areas of technology. I specifically liked working with one of the Analysts there. Many times, we exchange stories of different client interactions, military experiences, and information about the different projects we were working on. They inducted me into the world of the internet and web development. In fact, I remember us going over the ESPN home page and all the different elements of the page.
